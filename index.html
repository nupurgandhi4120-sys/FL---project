📚 Financial Literacy Corner

Master the basics of investing — one term at a time.

📄

Stock

A stock represents ownership in a company. When you buy a stock, you become a part-owner (shareholder) of that business.

🔖

Share

A share is a single unit of a stock. A company divides its total ownership into millions of shares that people can buy and sell.

💰

Investment

Putting your money into assets like stocks, bonds, or real estate with the expectation of earning a profit or income over time.

📈

Profit

The financial gain earned when the selling price of a stock is higher than the price at which it was purchased.

⚠️

Risk

The possibility that an investment's actual return will differ from the expected return, including the risk of losing some or all capital.

💸

Dividend

A portion of a company's profits distributed to its shareholders, usually paid quarterly, as a reward for holding the stock.
